Who We Are

Building resilience strategy capacity for a climate-accelerated world

Coastal cities and their stakeholders face growing, debilitating uncertainty that undermines resilience and sustainability planning. The Coastal Resilience Partnership equips public agencies, private sector leaders, and communities with the tools, frameworks, and networks to respond.


ARISE-US · Private Sector Alliance for Disaster Resilient Societies

Hosted under the ARISE-US global framework — the private sector constituency of the UN Office for Disaster Risk Reduction — the Partnership brings institutional credibility, global networks, and a proven resilience methodology to coastal risk.

Our Mission
To accelerate coastal resilience strategy development by connecting stakeholders with evidence-based tools, peer networks, blue finance pathways, and cross-sector capacity building.
The Challenge
From episodic hazard to deep uncertainty — cities now navigate non-linear, compound coastal risks with no probability ceiling, undermining planning across every time horizon.
The Response
A multi-generational, multi-sequence strategy spanning prevention, response, and recovery — adapting greenfield and brownfield assets, sites, and coastlines to stabilise communities and economies.

The Context

Why coastal resilience cannot wait

01
🌊

Accelerating Hazard

Sea-level rise, intensifying storm surge, and compound coastal hazards are exceeding design tolerances built into existing infrastructure and planning frameworks globally.

02
🔴

Deep Uncertainty

Risk models are breaking down. Non-linear cascades, compound events, and no probability ceiling on losses actively undermine confidence in conventional resilience planning.

03
🏙️

Governance Gaps

Most cities lack integrated coastal resilience strategies that span idiosyncratic asset-level risks and the systemic, ecosystem-level context within which those assets sit.

The Programme

Capacity building across all three phases of coastal resilience

Our programme equips city teams, private sector partners, and communities with structured tools, peer learning, and applied strategy development across prevention, response, and recovery.

Phase 01 · Prevention

Anticipate & Harden

Spatial risk assessment, nature-based coastal defence, greenfield siting controls, brownfield retrofit support, and policy and zoning reform frameworks.

Risk Mapping Asset Hardening Nature-Based Solutions Policy Frameworks
Phase 02 · Response

Absorb & Withstand

Early warning systems, critical infrastructure protection, community emergency preparedness, multi-agency coordination, and economic corridor continuity planning.

Early Warning Emergency Protocols Infrastructure Defence Coordination
Phase 03 · Recovery

Rebuild & Transform

Build-back-better standards, ecosystem rehabilitation, community stabilisation, adaptive coastal redesign, and knowledge integration into multi-sequence strategy.

Build Back Better Ecosystem Restoration Adaptive Redesign Knowledge Capture

Blue Finance Pathways

From scorecard gaps to bankable opportunities

The Scorecard creates a shared evidence base — translating coastal resilience gaps into structured investment opportunities across the full capital stack and all stakeholder types.

Adaptation Finance
Mature Companies & Assets
Blue bonds, sustainability-linked loans, and asset-level climate adaptation finance for coastal infrastructure retrofit, nature-based development, and hardening of legacy assets.
Venture & Growth Capital
Startups & Innovation
Equity and growth capital for nature-tech, coastal monitoring, climate insurtech, and risk analytics ventures operating at the frontier of coastal resilience solutions.
Concessional & Grants
Communities & Public Coastlines
Concessional finance, grants, and coastal ecosystem restoration bonds for public asset hardening, community resilience programmes, and social adaptation at the local level.
How the Scorecard Drives Capital
1

City Team Assessment

Multi-stakeholder team completes the 10-Essential Scorecard, scoring 1–5 across prevention, response, and recovery dimensions.

↓
2

Gap Analysis Output

Gaps below the 3.0 resilience threshold are identified, prioritised, and mapped to asset types, spatial areas, and communities.

↓
3

Use-of-Proceeds Design

Gaps are translated into structured blue finance use-of-proceeds frameworks, matched to the appropriate capital instruments and investor types.

↓
4

Investment Pipeline

Bankable projects are developed, connected to Partnership investors, and tracked against Scorecard improvement across the multi-sequence strategy.
